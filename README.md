# Decentralized Time Banking and Alternative Economy Network

A comprehensive blockchain-based system for building resilient local economies through time-based value exchange, community mutual aid, and economic democracy.

## Overview

This network consists of five interconnected smart contracts that enable communities to:

- Trade services based on time rather than money
- Build resilience through mutual aid networks
- Value and compensate care work
- Maintain stable local currencies
- Facilitate worker and community ownership

## Core Contracts

### 1. Time Exchange Contract (`time-exchange.clar`)
Enables peer-to-peer trading of services based on time units rather than monetary value. All services are valued equally by time spent.

**Key Features:**
- Service listing and discovery
- Time-based transactions
- Reputation tracking
- Dispute resolution

### 2. Community Resilience Contract (`community-resilience.clar`)
Facilitates mutual aid networks and emergency response systems within communities.

**Key Features:**
- Resource sharing pools
- Emergency assistance requests
- Community response coordination
- Resilience scoring

### 3. Care Work Valuation Contract (`care-work-valuation.clar`)
Recognizes and compensates traditionally unpaid care labor including childcare, eldercare, and community support.

**Key Features:**
- Care work registration
- Time tracking and validation
- Community-funded compensation
- Care provider support networks

### 4. Local Currency Stabilization Contract (`local-currency.clar`)
Maintains stability and value of community-based alternative currencies through algorithmic mechanisms.

**Key Features:**
- Currency issuance and burning
- Value stabilization mechanisms
- Exchange rate management
- Community treasury management

### 5. Economic Democracy Contract (`economic-democracy.clar`)
Enables worker and community ownership of enterprises through decentralized governance and profit sharing.

**Key Features:**
- Cooperative formation and management
- Democratic decision making
- Profit sharing mechanisms
- Worker ownership tracking

## Architecture

The system uses a modular architecture where each contract operates independently while sharing common data structures and principles:

- **Time Units**: Universal measure of value (1 hour = 1 time unit)
- **Community Membership**: Verified participants in the network
- **Reputation System**: Trust and reliability scoring
- **Democratic Governance**: Community-driven decision making

## Economic Principles

1. **Time Equality**: All human time is valued equally regardless of skill or education
2. **Mutual Aid**: Communities support each other through resource sharing
3. **Care Recognition**: Unpaid care work receives community compensation
4. **Local Resilience**: Reduced dependence on external economic systems
5. **Democratic Control**: Workers and communities own and control enterprises
